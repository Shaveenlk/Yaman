Train


The Sri Lankan train system is a popular mode of transportation that runs throughout the island nation of Sri Lanka. It offers an affordable and convenient way for locals and tourists to travel across the country. The system comprises of a network of railways that connect major cities and towns, offering both long-distance and short-distance travel options. Sri Lankan trains are known for their picturesque routes that showcase the country's stunning landscapes, including lush green hills, tea plantations, and scenic coastlines. The train system has several classes of travel, including first, second, and third class compartments. The trains are generally punctual and offer a comfortable and safe way to explore Sri Lanka.




Air Taxi


The Sri Lankan Airlines Air Taxi Service offers you a quick and breathtaking journey across the island to most of the popular destinations in Sri Lanka. The Air Taxi service is also available as a connecting flight from the international airport to your holiday destination. You can even charter a flight for a group of friends or your family and enjoy a private trip over paradise!




Tuk Tuks


Tuk-tuks are a popular mode of transportation in Sri Lanka, especially in cities and tourist areas. They are three-wheeled vehicles that can accommodate up to three passengers, and they are known for their maneuverability in traffic and narrow roads. Tuk-tuks are typically used for short distances, such as getting around town or going to nearby attractions. They are a convenient and affordable option, and many drivers are willing to negotiate the fare. However, it's important to negotiate the fare upfront to avoid any misunderstandings.




Bus


Sri Lanka has a well-developed bus service that covers almost every corner of the island. Buses are the most commonly used mode of transportation for both locals and tourists due to their affordability and accessibility. There are different types of buses in Sri Lanka, including standard buses, luxury buses, and intercity buses. The standard buses are the most common and inexpensive option, while luxury buses offer a more comfortable and spacious ride with air conditioning and sometimes even Wi-Fi. Intercity buses provide direct connections between major cities and towns, and they often run on a schedule.
