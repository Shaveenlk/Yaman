Water rafting


White water rafting in Sri Lanka is a thrilling adventure on the Kelani River in the Kithulgala area. The river offers a range of rapids, ranging from Grade 2 to 3, making it an ideal spot for beginners and intermediate rafters. The best time to go white water rafting in Sri Lanka is during the rainy season, from May to December. During this time, the water levels are high, and the rapids are more challenging, offering a more thrilling experience.




Safari


Safari in Sri Lanka is popular in national parks such as Yala, Udawalawe, and Wilpattu. Safaris are usually done using a jeep or a safari vehicle, accompanied by a qualified safari guide. The best time to go on a safari in Sri Lanka is during the early morning or late afternoon when the animals are most active. The cost of a safari in Sri Lanka ranges from $25 to $50 per person, and it is important to follow the rules and regulations of the park to ensure wildlife conservation.




Whale Watching


Whale watching in Sri Lanka is popular on the island's south coast, mainly in Mirissa and Trincomalee. The best time for whale watching in Mirissa is from November to April, while in Trincomalee, it is from May to October. During these months, visitors can observe whales, dolphins, and other marine life in their natural habitat. The cost of whale watching in Sri Lanka varies depending on the tour operator and the trip duration. Choosing a reputable and experienced tour operator is important to ensure safe and responsible tourism practices.




Surfing


Surfing in Sri Lanka is a popular water sport that takes place along the south and east coasts of the island. The best time for surfing in Sri Lanka is from November to April on the south coast and from May to October on the east coast. There are several popular surf spots in Sri Lanka, such as Hikkaduwa, Arugam Bay, and Mirissa. The cost of surfing in Sri Lanka varies depending on the location and the duration of the surf lesson. Choosing a reputable surf school and following safety guidelines is important to ensure a safe and enjoyable surfing experience.
